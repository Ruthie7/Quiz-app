Week 1 — Awareness
1. Find Your Lazy Archetype
Day 1 · 15 min read

Before you can change, you need to know exactly what type of "lazy" you are. Laziness isn't one thing — it shows up differently in different people, and the fix is different for each type.

The 4 Archetypes

  • The Drifter — Has potential but no direction. Needs clarity before habits.
  • The Starter — Begins everything, finishes nothing. Needs momentum systems.
  • The Ghost — Avoids the work by staying invisible. Needs micro-wins.
  • The Perfectionist — Won't start unless it's perfect. Needs "done > perfect" reps.
Today's Action

Read each archetype above. Which one made you uncomfortable because it's too accurate? Write it down. That's your starting point.

```
2. The Time Audit
Day 2–3 · 20 min exercise

You cannot manage time you haven't tracked. Most people have no idea where their hours actually go — they just feel busy and produce nothing.

How to do it

  • For 2 days, track everything you do in 30-minute blocks
  • Write it down as it happens — not from memory later
  • At the end, highlight the blocks that produced real output
  • Circle the blocks that were pure distraction or drift

What you're looking for

  • Your top 3 time leaks (usually phone, YouTube, fake productivity)
  • Your natural high-energy windows (morning? late night?)
  • How many hours are truly available for focused work
Today's Action

Start tracking right now. Set a timer for every 30 minutes today and log what you did. No judgment — just data.

3. Kill Your 3 Biggest Time Leaks
Day 4–5 · Action-based

Now that you know where your time goes, it's time to remove the 3 biggest thieves. Not reduce — remove. At least for 30 days.

Common time leaks

  • Mindless phone scrolling (Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X)
  • Checking notifications every few minutes
  • Watching content without intention (YouTube rabbit holes)
  • "Researching" instead of doing
  • Long unproductive conversations that go nowhere

The removal protocol

  • Delete the apps (not just log out — delete)
  • Put your phone in another room during work hours
  • Set app time limits to 15 min/day max for the next 30 days
Today's Action

Write down your 3 biggest time leaks from your audit. Then take one physical action right now to remove or limit each one.
```

Week 2 — Environment
4. Design Your Environment for Success
Day 8 · 30 min exercise

Willpower runs out. Your environment is permanent. High performers don't rely on discipline — they design their surroundings so the right behavior is automatic.

The 4 environment rules

  • Make good habits obvious — Put your book on your pillow. Put your workout shoes by the door.
  • Make bad habits invisible — Phone in another room. Remove junk food from the house.
  • Make good habits easy — Lay out your gym clothes the night before. Pre-write tomorrow's Big 3.
  • Make bad habits hard — Log out of Netflix. Delete social media apps daily.
Today's Action

Spend 30 minutes physically rearranging your space. Remove 3 things that enable distraction. Add 3 visual cues for your goals.

```
5. Install Your Morning Non-Negotiable
Day 10 · Ongoing from here

How you start your morning sets the tone for everything. A strong morning isn't about waking up at 5am — it's about owning the first 60 minutes before the world demands your attention.

Build your morning stack (pick 3–4)

  • No phone for first 30 minutes after waking
  • Drink water immediately upon waking
  • 5–10 minutes of movement or stretching
  • Write your Big 3 for the day
  • 10 minutes of reading (not social media)
  • One affirmation or intention for the day

The rule

  • Your morning stack must be done before you check your phone
  • Start small — 3 items max if you're new to this
  • Same stack every day. Consistency over complexity.
Today's Action

Write your morning stack right now. 3–4 items. Tomorrow, wake up and do them before touching your phone. That's it.

Week 3–4 — Momentum
6. The 10-Minute Rule
Day 15 · Use forever

Resistance is highest before you start. Once you're 10 minutes into a task, the friction is gone and momentum takes over. The 10-minute rule exploits this biology.

How it works

  • When you don't want to start something, tell yourself: "Just 10 minutes."
  • Set a timer and begin — no pressure to continue after
  • 90% of the time, you'll keep going past 10 minutes
  • The other 10% — 10 minutes is still better than zero

Where to use it

  • Starting your most important task of the day
  • Getting back to work after a break
  • Any task you've been avoiding for days
Today's Action

Pick the task you've been avoiding most. Set a 10-minute timer right now and start. Don't think — just start the timer.
```

Week 5–6 — Systems
7. The Big 3 Daily Framework
Day 31 · Use daily from here

Most people try to do everything and accomplish nothing. The Big 3 system forces you to identify the three tasks that — if completed — make the day a win. Everything else is secondary.

The rules

  • Every morning, write exactly 3 tasks — no more
  • Task 1 must be your most important/hardest task (do it first)
  • All 3 must be completable in one day
  • A "good day" = all 3 done. A "great day" = Big 3 done before noon.

How to choose your Big 3

  • Ask: "What would make today feel like a win?"
  • Prioritize tasks that move your main goal forward
  • Avoid tasks that feel productive but produce no output
Today's Action

Open the Planner tab and write your Big 3 for today right now. Start with #1 immediately after.

```
8. Deep Work Block Architecture
Day 35 · 25 min read

Deep work is the ability to focus on cognitively demanding tasks without distraction. It's the skill that separates high earners from average performers — and it's trainable.

Building your deep work blocks

  • Start with 90-minute blocks (the natural ultradian rhythm)
  • Schedule deep work during your peak energy window
  • Phone off or in another room — non-negotiable
  • One task per block. No multitasking.
  • After the block: a real break (walk, not scroll)

The ramp-up protocol

  • Week 1: One 45-min deep work block per day
  • Week 2: One 90-min block per day
  • Week 3+: Two 90-min blocks per day
Today's Action

Block out your deep work window in your calendar for the next 7 days. Treat it like a meeting you cannot miss.

9. Identity-Based Habit Stacking
Day 40 · Mindset shift

Habits stick when they're tied to identity, not outcomes. "I'm trying to read more" fails. "I'm a reader" succeeds. Every action you take is a vote for who you believe you are.

How to stack habits on identity

  • Choose the identity first: "I am someone who..."
  • Find the smallest action that proves that identity
  • Do it consistently until it feels automatic
  • Layer the next habit on top once the first is locked in

Habit stack examples

  • After I pour my morning coffee → I write my Big 3
  • After I sit at my desk → I start my deep work timer
  • After I brush my teeth at night → I read for 20 minutes
Today's Action

Write 3 "After I ___ → I will ___" habit stacks. Make them specific and attach them to things you already do every day.
```

Week 7–8 — Energy
10. Energy Management Protocol
Day 48 · Ongoing

Time management is dead. Energy management is the real game. You can have all the time in the world but if your energy is shot, you'll produce nothing. Protect your energy like money.

The 4 energy pillars

  • Sleep — 7–8 hours non-negotiable. Everything else is built on this.
  • Movement — 20–30 min daily. Walks count. This doubles your focus capacity.
  • Nutrition — Avoid sugar and heavy carbs before deep work. They crash your focus.
  • Recovery — Real breaks between work blocks. No scrolling during breaks.

Energy audit questions

  • What time of day do I feel sharpest?
  • What drains my energy fastest?
  • Am I sleeping enough, or lying to myself?
Today's Action

Rate your current energy in the Planner. Then identify your #1 energy drain and make one change today to address it.

Week 9–10 — Standards
11. Never Miss Twice
Day 61 · Rule to live by

Missing once is an accident. Missing twice is the start of a new (bad) habit. The goal of this phase is not perfection — it's resilience. You will have bad days. What separates legends from quitters is what happens the next day.

The Never Miss Twice protocol

  • If you miss a habit → forgive immediately, no guilt spiral
  • The next day is a non-negotiable comeback day
  • Scale back if needed (5 min instead of 30) but never skip entirely
  • Failure is data, not identity. Reset at midnight.

What to do after a bad day

  • Write down what caused the miss (be honest)
  • Identify one system change to prevent it next time
  • Wake up the next day and do the minimum viable version
Today's Action

Think of a habit you've missed recently. Write down what caused it and one change you'll make. Then commit to the next day no matter what.

```
12. The Scorecard System
Day 65 · Weekly accountability

What gets measured gets managed. The scorecard is a simple weekly score out of 10 that keeps you honest about how you're actually performing — not how you feel you're performing.

How to score your week

  • Count how many days you completed your Big 3 (out of 7)
  • Count your habit tracker completion rate
  • Rate your energy levels for the week (1–10)
  • Calculate a simple average — that's your weekly score

What the scores mean

  • 8–10: Excellent week. Maintain and slightly increase challenge.
  • 6–7: Decent. Identify the 1 thing that held you back.
  • Below 6: Something is wrong. Review your systems, not your willpower.
Today's Action

Go to the Review tab and score this past week using the scorecard system. Be brutally honest — the number is for you, not anyone else.
```

Week 11–12 — Peak Performance
13. Build Your Peak Performance Loop
Day 75 · Final system

By now your habits are forming. This lesson is about wiring them together into a self-reinforcing loop that runs automatically — so staying consistent becomes easier than stopping.

The peak performance loop

  • Cue → Something that triggers your habit (alarm, location, event)
  • Routine → The habit itself (your morning stack, deep work, etc.)
  • Reward → Something that makes the habit satisfying (track it, celebrate it)
  • Review → Weekly reflection that tightens the loop

Sustaining it beyond 90 days

  • Keep the weekly review as a forever ritual
  • Run a new 90-day sprint every quarter
  • Raise the standard slightly each sprint — never plateau
Today's Action

Map out your complete daily loop on paper: morning → deep work → evening shutdown. Identify any gaps and fill them.

```
14. The 90-Day Reflection
Day 90 · Milestone

You made it to Day 90. This is the reflection lesson — the most important one. Not because of what's in it, but because the fact that you're reading it means you stayed the course.

Reflection questions

  • Who were you on Day 1 vs. who are you now?
  • What was the single biggest shift in how you operate?
  • What habit had the most impact on your life?
  • What is one thing you still want to change?
  • What does your next 90-day sprint look like?

What comes next

  • Rest for 3–5 days. You've earned it.
  • Then design your next 90-day sprint with raised standards
  • Share your transformation — it will inspire someone else to start
Today's Action

Write answers to all 5 reflection questions in the Review tab. Then plan your next sprint. Legends don't stop — they level up.
```
